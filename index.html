Ran Ji (Sara)

Hi! I'm a Psychology student at UC San Diego (2025–2027), with double BAs in Psychology and Cognitive Science from UC Davis (2023–2025). I'm interested in child development, early autism detection, inclusive education, and AI cognition—metacognition and perspective-taking in vision–language models.

I work with the VBVR Team, Grow AI, and previously the ASPIRE Program at the MIND Institute; I also do research at Thriving Asians on Asian mental health. I care about how children and AI both learn to understand the world—and how we can support them better.

Research Interests

  • Metacognition and egocentric bias in vision–language models
  • Video reasoning and benchmark design for AI
  • Infant gesture, vocabulary, and early ASD screening
  • Asian mental health and help-seeking
  • Social-emotional learning and inclusive education

Projects

  • VBVR: A Very Big Video Reasoning Suite
    Co-designed reasoning tasks and a 200-task data generator; contributed to benchmark design and model evaluation; built the project website.
    video-reason.com
  • Grow AI: Metacognition & Egocentric Bias in VLMs
    Two-stage protocol (perceptual judgment + confidence) for visual tasks; evaluated 103 VLMs on perspective-taking with custom images.
    Grow AI
  • ASPIRE: What Parents Notice — Gesture & Vocabulary
    Behavioral coding of infant social-communicative behaviors (ECI scale), MIND Institute with Prof. Meagan Talbott; independent project on parental concerns and communicative outcomes.
    ASPIRE Program
  • Stepping Stones SEL
    Designed and delivered a 6-week social-emotional learning curriculum for grades 1–2 in Shanghai; role-playing, emotional drawing, and support for inclusive participation.
  • AGI as Second Being: The Structural-Generative Ontology of Intelligence
    Maijunxian Wang, Ran Ji (2025).
