Ran Ji (Sara)

Hi! I'm Ran Ji (Sara). I'm pursuing a BS in Psychology at UC San Diego (2025–2027) and hold double BAs in Psychology and Cognitive Science from UC Davis (2023–2025). I'm interested in child development, early autism detection, inclusive education, and AI cognition—e.g., metacognition and perspective-taking in vision–language models.

I work with the VBVR Team on video reasoning benchmarks, with Grow AI on metacognition and egocentric bias in VLMs, and previously with the ASPIRE Program at the MIND Institute on infant gesture and vocabulary. I also do research at Thriving Asians on Asian mental health and serve as Curriculum Co-Designer for a school in Shanghai.

Contact: r9ji@ucsd.edu · La Jolla, CA

Research Interests

  • Metacognition and egocentric bias in vision–language models (Grow AI)
  • Video reasoning and benchmark design for AI (VBVR)
  • Infant gesture, vocabulary, and caregiver concerns; early ASD screening (ASPIRE / MIND Institute)
  • Asian mental health, stigma, and help-seeking (Thriving Asians)
  • Social-emotional learning and inclusive education (Stepping Stones, psychodrama)

Selected Projects

  • VBVR: A Very Big Video Reasoning Suite (Nov 2025 – Present)
    Research team member. Co-designed reasoning tasks and a 200-task data generator, contributed to dataset generation, benchmark design, model evaluation, and built the project website video-reason.com with internal analytics.
  • Grow AI: Metacognition in VLMs & Egocentric Bias (Apr – Nov 2025)
    Metacognition: Co-designed a two-stage protocol (perceptual judgment + confidence rating) for three visual tasks; generated 2,600+ stimuli (Python/Cursor), collected 16,000+ data points across six models (Qwen-VL, Gemma, etc.).
    Egocentric bias: Evaluated egocentric bias in 103 VLMs with 28 custom images and analyzed perspective-taking abilities.
  • ASPIRE: What Parents Notice — Gesture & Vocabulary (Jan – Sep 2025)
    Role: Student Researcher, MIND Institute (Prof. Meagan Talbott). Behavioral coding of infant social-communicative behaviors (ECI scale), managed MCDI and observational data. Led an independent project linking parental concerns to infant communicative outcomes; contributed to lab coding protocol refinements.
  • Stepping Stones SEL Volunteer (Jul – Aug 2025)
    Designed and delivered a 6-week social-emotional learning curriculum for grades 1–2 (Shanghai). Used role-playing, situational games, and emotional drawing; supported a visually impaired child from withdrawal to active participation.
