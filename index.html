Ran Ji (Sara)

Hi! I'm Ran Ji (also known as Sara), a Psychology and Cognitive Science major at UC Davis, passionate about child development, early autism detection, and inclusive education. I currently work with the MIND Institute, Grow AI and ASPIRE Program on projects involving neurodiverse populations, including autism and ADHD.

I'm particularly interested in how children understand emotions, attribute intentions to AI and humans, and construct meaning through interaction. I've also served as a special education classroom assistant, where I supported neurodiverse learners aged 6 to 21.

Research Interests

  • Early autism detection and family-based intervention (based on TEDI Lab research experience)
  • Children's social cognitive development and emotion regulation (based on developmental psychology coursework)
  • AI psychological attribution and metacognitive research (based on Grow AI interdisciplinary project)
  • Social-emotional learning and inclusive education (based on Stepping Stones teaching practice)
  • Cross-cultural educational psychology applications (based on US-China educational background)

Selected Projects

  • AI Metacognitive Performance Research (2025.04-present)
    Context: Joined Grow AI interdisciplinary research team to explore whether AI can develop intelligence like children
    Task: Designed experiments testing metacognitive performance of mainstream VLMs (Ovis2, Qwen2.5, etc.)
    Action: Applied cognitive psychology theories to construct Grid, Gabor, and Color visual tasks with two-stage experimental framework
    Result: Completed two paper drafts, enhanced interdisciplinary research capabilities, understood psychology's application value in AI field
  • TEDI Lab Early Intervention Research (2025.01-2025.09)
    Context: Joined MIND Institute through ASPIRE program to study infant social behavior
    Task: Remote assessment of 6-36 month infants' social and sensory behaviors, conducted behavioral coding analysis
    Action: Applied developmental psychology theories to analyze infant social referencing behaviors, participated in reliability analysis
    Result: Independently completed interview coding, understood rigor of psychological research, deepened understanding of early intervention importance
  • Campus Emotion Support System Design (2025 Spring)
    Context: UWP 104D course group project designing educational grant proposal
    Task: Wrote "Best Buddies: Peer-Led Sensory Wellness" cross-grade SEL project
    Action: Applied psychology theories to design high school psychology students guiding elementary students' emotion regulation activities
    Result: Completed 10-page formal proposal, learned to transform psychological theories into practical projects
  • Stepping Stones Social-Emotional Learning Project (2025.07-08)
    Context: Joined Shanghai elementary SEL project as volunteer lead teacher
    Task: Designed 6-week emotion-themed curriculum including emotion recognition, expression, and regulation training
    Action: Applied emotion regulation theories to design "emotion thermometer" and "emotion mask" activities
    Result: Students' emotion expression abilities significantly improved, experienced direct value of psychology knowledge in improving children's learning states
